# Product

## Register

product

## Users
FEU Tech students, PyTorch campus officers, department leads, and chapter executives use this platform during campus activity planning, event enrollment, member development, and career-readiness review. Students need a personal hub for skills, event access, social-source linking, merit progress, and leaderboards. Officers and executives need dense operational dashboards for events, member analytics, approvals, and growth signals.

## Product Purpose
PyTorch FEU Tech Campus Platform is a community intelligence hub. It turns member activity, events, validated achievements, and curated public profile data into normalized career intelligence. Success means members understand their growth path, officers can run chapter operations with less manual tracking, and public outputs never become the source of truth.

## Brand Personality
Focused, technical, campus-proud. The product should feel like a serious engineering command center with a warm PyTorch signal: dark, precise, energetic, and trustworthy without becoming decorative.

## Anti-references
Avoid generic SaaS hero pages, AI-dashboard cliches, over-purple gradient surfaces, gamified toy interfaces, and public leaderboard designs that expose private student data. Avoid forms that imply real credential capture before Supabase Auth and RLS are wired.

## Design Principles
- Normalize first: every screen should imply structured data behind generated views.
- Privacy is visible: locked panels, consent labels, and public-safe handles must distinguish private from public data.
- Officer workflows stay dense: admin pages prioritize scanning, filtering, and action over marketing polish.
- Member growth feels earned: merit, streaks, and ranks should explain progress without shaming lower-activity users.
- AI assists, humans approve: generated recommendations and scraped signals remain reviewable, not silently authoritative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation, visible focus states, reduced-motion fallbacks, and responsive layouts for mobile, tablet, and desktop. Avoid relying on color alone for role gates, event status, or lock states.
